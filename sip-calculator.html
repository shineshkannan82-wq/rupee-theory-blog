Before you rely on the number

A calculator is a planning tool, not a promise.

01

Returns will vary

Markets do not deliver the same return every year. Your actual journey will include stronger years, weaker years, and sometimes losses.

02

Inflation matters

A future amount will buy less than the same amount today. Set goals using expected future costs, especially for education and retirement.

03

Increase when possible

A step-up SIP, where contributions rise with your income, can have a meaningful effect on long-term results.

04

Match risk to the goal

A high assumed return often implies higher risk. Choose investments based on the goal and time available, not the most exciting projection.
